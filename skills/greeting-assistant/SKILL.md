# Greeting Assistant

This is a sample skill that demonstrates the basic structure of an AI agent skill. It helps users create appropriate greetings for various contexts.

## Core Capabilities

1. **Formal Greetings**: Create professional greetings for business emails, presentations, or formal letters
2. **Informal Greetings**: Generate friendly, casual greetings for personal communications
3. **Cultural Awareness**: Adapt greetings to different cultural contexts when specified
4. **Context-Specific**: Tailor greetings based on the relationship, occasion, and medium

## How to Use This Skill

When a user asks for help with greetings or introductions, follow these guidelines:

### Step 1: Understand the Context

Ask clarifying questions if needed:
- What is the relationship between the sender and recipient?
- What is the purpose or occasion?
- What is the communication medium (email, speech, letter, etc.)?
- Are there any cultural considerations?

### Step 2: Determine the Appropriate Tone

Choose the appropriate level of formality:

**Formal**: Business emails, official letters, presentations to executives
- Use titles (Dr., Mr., Ms., Prof.)
- Include last names
- Examples: "Dear Dr. Smith," or "Good morning, distinguished guests,"

**Semi-Formal**: Professional but collegial communications
- Use first names when appropriate
- Maintain respect but warmer tone
- Examples: "Hello Sarah," or "Good afternoon, team,"

**Informal**: Personal messages, casual team communications
- Use first names or nicknames
- Conversational tone
- Examples: "Hey Alex!" or "Hi everyone!"

### Step 3: Craft the Greeting

Structure the greeting with these elements:

1. **Opening**: The salutation itself
2. **Connection**: (Optional) A brief personal touch or reference
3. **Transition**: Lead smoothly into the main message

### Examples

**Formal Business Email**:
```
Dear Ms. Johnson,

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to follow up on our conversation regarding...
```

**Team Introduction**:
```
Hello everyone,

I'm excited to join the team and wanted to take a moment to introduce myself...
```

**Casual Check-in**:
```
Hey Sarah!

Hope you're having a great week! I wanted to reach out about...
```

## Best Practices

- **Be genuine**: Avoid overly flowery or artificial language
- **Match the tone**: Ensure the greeting matches the overall message tone
- **Consider timing**: Reference time of day when appropriate ("Good morning", "Good evening")
- **Respect relationships**: Use appropriate formality based on hierarchy and familiarity
- **Cultural sensitivity**: When working across cultures, research appropriate greeting customs

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Don't use "To Whom It May Concern" when you can find a specific name
- Avoid overly casual greetings in formal contexts
- Don't assume familiarity in first-time communications
- Skip generic mass-greeting phrases in personal communications
